San Bernardo Cableway - This place is one of the most iconic places in the city. You'll be able to ride in a very comfortable cable way, aving an amazing view of the center of the city, and the perfect opportunity to take the most amazing pictures of the city.

San Bernardo Hill - A hill right next to the city. Here you'll find some of the best restaurants in the city, as well as some beautiful views of the entire city, that you can enjoy and carry in your memory the rest of your life.

Salta's Cathedral - Rebuit after being destroyed by an earthquake in the middle of the 20th century, this building is a symbol of the history of a people full offaith, willing to protect their own even during the harshest circumstances.

Cabildo de Salta - Currently known as the Historical Museum of the North, the Cabildo used to be the government house back during the colonial days. It was built together with the Cathedral and the main square, and it remains one of the main 'must visit' places in the city.

Casa de Güemes - This was the original house of the biggest hero in the history of the city. Martin Güemes was a local army captain, that during the Independence Wars decided to recruit and train local men to defend the frontier, protecting one of the most important roads leading to the main towns of the colonies, ensuring the victory against the Spanish army.

La Virgen del Cerro - In this hill, a local woman called Maria Livia claimed she had several manifestations of the Virgin Mary. After several supernatural experiences, she claimed to hear a celestial voice, which lead her to share her experiences with her aquaintances. Today there is a sanctuary built in a beautiful hill, which you can visit and enjoy.
